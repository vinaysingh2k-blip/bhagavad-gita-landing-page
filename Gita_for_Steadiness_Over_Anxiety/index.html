Four Core Teachings

Four Chapters, Four Verses, Four Immediate Practices

A preview of how each chapter works — a real moment named, a cited verse, and one practical exercise.

🫨

The Anatomy of Worry

Ch. 1 · Bhagavad Gita 1.30

Naming your state as precisely as Arjuna names his — not just "anxious," but exactly what your body and thoughts are doing — is itself a step toward steadying it.

🌬️

Breath as the Anchor

Ch. 3 · Bhagavad Gita 5.27

When a racing mind can't be reasoned with directly, the breath is the more accessible lever. Six slow breaths, exhale longer than inhale, before you try to think it through.

🤲

The Anxiety of Attachment

Ch. 5 · Bhagavad Gita 2.62

Anxiety often tracks the tightness of your grip on an outcome more than the outcome itself. Loosening the grip, without abandoning the desire, tends to ease the anxiety proportionally.

🕊️

Surrender as Relief, Not Defeat

Ch. 11 · Bhagavad Gita 18.66

Surrender isn't giving up on the outcome — it's putting down the parts that were never yours to carry alone. That release is where real, structural relief comes from.

🕉️

"There is no wisdom for the unsteady, nor meditation; without meditation there is no peace, and without peace, how can there be happiness?"

— Bhagavad Gita 2.66
