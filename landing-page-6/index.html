Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a translation of the Gita?

No. Gita for Energy & Health is an original, contemporary commentary inspired by the themes of the Bhagavad Gita. It is not a translation of the Gita and does not reproduce any copyrighted translation of the source text. Each chapter cites one specific, publicly known verse by chapter and verse number, then offers original commentary, a practical exercise, and reflection questions applied to the body.

Is this a diet plan or workout program?

No. It's a book of teachings and small practices, not a meal plan or fitness routine. Each chapter offers one idea inspired by the Gita and one no-equipment exercise you can start the same day.

Why only 12 chapters and not a full translation?

This companion distills twelve ideas from the Gita most directly useful for the body — food, breath, sleep, movement, and steadiness under stress — into short, usable chapters, rather than reproducing the complete eighteen-chapter text.

Anyone whose body runs on inherited habits, quick fixes, and low-grade neglect — people who want a steadier, less punishing way to think about food, sleep, movement, and stress.
