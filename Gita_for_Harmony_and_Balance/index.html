Four Pillars of Harmony

Four Ways the Gita Steadies a Restless Mind

Real moments of overwhelm, mapped to the chapters that speak to them directly.

🫀

Name the Feeling First

Chapter I · 1.28–29

Arjuna's collapse is a picture anyone recognizes — a big decision suddenly feels unbearable. Naming the feeling honestly is the first real step before wisdom can be heard.

⚖️

Balance in Life

Chapter II · 2.47

Commit fully to your work while releasing your grip on how it turns out — give your best effort, then let go of anxious checking and comparison.

🪷

Purpose in Action

Chapter V · 5.10

Full participation in life, without the residue of grasping sticking to you afterward — a daily check-in: am I acting, or am I clinging to the result?

🕊️

Peace Within

Chapter VI · 6.5

Self-mastery is less about intensity and more about patient, repeated practice over time — a steady seat, a quiet place, moderate habits.
